> _The Empire was not born from conquest, but from exhaustion._

## Overview

The **Thalveran Empire** is one of the oldest surviving political institutions on [[Thalvëra\|Thalvëra]]. It was founded after the [[War of Dragons\|War of Dragons]], when the peoples of the continent sought unity in the ruins left by dragons, gods, and centuries of war.

For a time, the Empire held nearly all of Thalvëra beneath one political order. Today, it survives mostly as a name, a law, and a memory.

## Origins

The Empire rose from the ashes of the [[War of Dragons\|War of Dragons]]. Its first purpose was not conquest, but survival.

Kings, princes, elven lords, sea-lords, temple authorities, and military leaders swore themselves to a shared imperial order. The founding idea was simple: Thalvëra would no longer wait for gods or dragons to hold the world together.

## The Age of Imperial Unity

In its early centuries, the Empire became the shield of the continent. Imperial roads, shared laws, fortified routes, and common military structures helped the peoples of Thalvëra rebuild.

At its height, the Empire included nearly the entire continent, though not every realm belonged to it in the same way. Some regions were loyal provinces, others were treaty-bound allies, and a few were almost autonomous from the beginning.

## The Long Unraveling

The Empire did not collapse in a single war. It loosened slowly, as distance, old pride, and local ambition pulled its realms apart.

The first great fracture came from Ryvia. Its southern position and mountain barriers made it difficult for the imperial heartlands to control, and over time the region drifted from imperial authority.

Later, Ryvia itself divided into [[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Thalvëra/Kingdom of Myramar\|Myramar]] and [[Kingdom Aldemar\|Aldemar]]. What had once been a southern imperial realm became a divided region of rival crowns, maritime interests, and local ambitions.

## The Old Elven Domain

The [[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Thalvëra/Old Elven Domain\|Old Elven Domain]] never fully submitted to imperial rule. Its rulers acknowledged the Empire when necessary, but governed their own affairs according to older laws and older memories.

To many elves, the Empire was not eternal. It was another mortal age passing through the world.

## The Aegeryn Sea League

The [[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Thalvëra/Aegeryn Sea League\|Aegeryn Sea League]] remained tied to the Empire through treaties and trade. Its ships, ports, and sea laws increasingly served league interests before imperial ones.

Today, the League still sends envoys to imperial gatherings. It rarely sends obedience.

## The Empire Today

Today, the Empire is held together by three remaining core kingdoms: [[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Thalvëra/Kingdom of Eryndor\|Eryndor]], [[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Thalvëra/Kingdom of Thalanor\|Thalanor]], and [[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Thalvëra/Kingdom of Kharindor\|Kharindor]]. Each kingdom governs itself, keeps its own laws, and guards its own ambitions.

They still recognize the imperial structure, but rarely agree on what that recognition means. The imperial banner still flies, but it no longer commands the continent as it once did.

## The Imperial Throne

The Empire still has an emperor, though most nobles consider the position weak and ceremonial. The emperor signs decrees, receives envoys, presides over old rituals, and embodies the idea that Thalvëra is still one realm.

This may be a mistake. Behind the quiet of the throne, the emperor has learned to use favors, debts, archives, forgotten laws, and old loyalties.

The throne has little visible power, but it remembers things the kingdoms would rather forget.

## The High Imperial Council

**The High Imperial Council** is the highest governing assembly of the Empire. It was founded after the [[War of Dragons\|War of Dragons]] to give the realms of Thalvëra a shared political voice.

In its first centuries, many powers held seats in the Council: kings, elven lords, sea-league envoys, temple delegates, magistrates, and wardens of the old roads. Today, most of those seats are empty, ceremonial, or ignored.

The Council is now dominated by [[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Thalvëra/Kingdom of Eryndor\|Eryndor]], [[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Thalvëra/Kingdom of Thalanor\|Thalanor]], and [[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Thalvëra/Kingdom of Kharindor\|Kharindor]]. It can delay almost anything, prevent almost anyone from acting, and decide almost nothing.

## Politics and Tensions

The three remaining kingdoms still use the imperial structure when it benefits them. They invoke imperial law against rivals, appeal to tradition in diplomacy, and use the High Imperial Council as a stage for influence, yet none of them truly wish to submit to central rule.

This creates a constant state of paralysis within the Empire:

- **[[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Thalvëra/Kingdom of Eryndor\|Eryndor]]** seeks to preserve its old prestige and its traditional closeness to the imperial throne.
- **[[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Thalvëra/Kingdom of Thalanor\|Thalanor]]** pushes for greater military influence and stronger authority over border defense.
- **[[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Thalvëra/Kingdom of Kharindor\|Kharindor]]** guards its maritime interests and resists any attempt to limit its independence.
- **The Emperor** appears weak, but quietly gathers favors, debts, and forgotten laws.
- **The High Imperial Council** argues endlessly, able to delay almost anything but decide almost nothing.

The Empire survives because no kingdom wants to be blamed for ending it. It weakens because no kingdom truly wishes to save it.

## Legacy

The Thalveran Empire is no longer the power it once was, but its roads, laws, seals, coins, ceremonies, and titles still shape life across the continent.

To some, it remains the last promise of unity. To others, it is a dead crown casting a long shadow.
