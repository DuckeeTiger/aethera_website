# The Short Story of Aethera

> [!class-recite|warlock]
>
> *“The world did not survive because the gods won.*  
> *It survived because they chose to become the veil between creation and the void.”*

---

### Before Creation

Before stars burned in the heavens and before worlds carried names, there existed only the Eidolons.

The Eidolons were not gods, nor demons, nor living beings as mortals understand them. They were ancient cosmic intelligences born from the void before creation itself. Across eternity they shaped worlds from chaos, allowed them to flourish, and eventually erased them once they had grown stagnant or complete.

To the Eidolons, destruction was not evil. It was balance.

Civilizations rose and vanished. Entire realities lived and died in endless cycles. The Eidolons believed this was the natural order of existence. Creation was meant to be temporary. Permanence itself was an aberration.

---

### The Two Who Remembered

Among the countless beings born within those forgotten cycles were two entities unlike any before them:

Racelion, the First Light, embodiment of harmony and order, and Viraen, the Wild Heart, embodiment of creation and change.

Unlike all others, they remembered the worlds that had been lost.

They remembered civilizations erased by the void. They remembered songs that no longer existed. They remembered grief.

Again and again they survived the destruction of worlds, fleeing from one dying reality into the next while carrying fragments of memory with them. Over countless cycles they came to understand a terrible truth:

> The Eidolons would never allow creation to endure forever.

And yet, despite this, they refused to stop creating.

Aethera was not their first world. It was merely the latest and greatest of countless attempts to preserve life from inevitable destruction.

---

### The Secret Creation of Aethera

Far beyond the reach of the known void, Racelion and Viraen created a hidden world and named it Aethera.

At its center stood the first great realm: Racriaven. A world where divine order and wild creation existed together in harmony. Mountains rose from sleeping stone, forests spread across endless valleys, and seas formed beneath newborn stars.

For the first time in ages, Racelion and Viraen believed they had escaped the cycle.

But they also understood that they alone could not protect this new creation forever.

So they divided fragments of their own divine essence and shaped twelve gods to stand beside them. Six followed the ideals of Racelion, while six walked the path of Viraen. Together they formed the first pantheon of Aethera.

The gods walked openly among mortals. They taught the first civilizations how to build, write, shape magic, and understand the world around them. Faith, knowledge, and kingdoms were born beneath divine guidance.

For a brief age, Aethera knew peace.

---

### [[The Shattering War\|The Shattering War]]

No world could remain hidden forever.

Eventually, the [[Eidolons\|Eidolons]] discovered Aethera, and with their arrival began the great cosmic conflict later remembered as the Shattering War.

The [[Eidolons\|Eidolons]] did not seek conquest. They sought correction.

To them, Racelion and Viraen had committed the ultimate sin: they had created something meant to endure.

The war lasted for thousands of years. Entire civilizations rose and vanished within its shadow. Generations of mortals were born into a world already burning, knowing only ruin, fear, and survival.

The gods fought beside their creations while the [[Eidolons\|Eidolons]] unleashed horrors from beyond reality itself. Continents cracked beneath divine power. The skies burned. The boundaries between worlds weakened.

It was during this age that mortals first learned to wield magic, not as art or scholarship, but as a weapon necessary for survival.

And in desperation, Racelion and Viraen created new living weapons to aid the war effort:

The dragons.

Forged from divine essence and primal elemental power, dragons became the greatest creations of the old gods. Some embodied wisdom and guardianship, while others inherited pride, hunger, and ambition. Even among dragonkind, division was inevitable.

---

### The Sundering of the Pantheon

As the war dragged on, the [[Eidolons\|Eidolons]] realized they could not destroy Aethera through force alone.

Instead, they turned toward doubt.

They whispered to [[Viraen\|Viraen]] of all the worlds that had already fallen. They showed her the endless inevitability of the cycle and the suffering caused by endlessly prolonging creation against its natural end.

Slowly, [[Viraen\|Viraen]] began to lose faith.

Her six followers turned with her, dividing the pantheon against itself. Now creation warred not only against the void, but against its own fractured soul.

At the same time, another influence entered the world.

Three dark entities emerged within the chaos of the war: [[Bhaal\|Bhaal]], [[Myrkul\|Myrkul]], and [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Bane\|Bane]]. Unlike the other gods, they were not born from Racelion or Viraen. They were fragments of the Eidolons’ will made manifest within reality itself, sent to corrupt, weaken, and infect the world from within.

Through them, the influence of the [[Eidolons\|Eidolons]] lingered even where the gods still held power.

---

### The Birth of the [[EN/Lore/History of Aethera/Worldveil\|Wordveil]]

At the edge of total annihilation, [[Racelion\|Racelion]] and [[Viraen\|Viraen]] understood one final truth:

Neither order nor chaos alone could preserve the world.

Only unity could.

In the final battle of [[The Shattering War\|The Shattering War]], the two creators abandoned their physical forms and merged their divine essence together. Their union became the Worldveil, also known as the Veilweave or the Világszövedék: a vast metaphysical lattice woven from harmony and wild creation alike.

The [[EN/Lore/History of Aethera/Worldveil\|Worldveil]] did not destroy the [[Eidolons\|Eidolons]].

Instead, it concealed and protected what remained of reality from them.

But the act came at terrible cost.

The force of their union shattered Aethera completely. The once-unified world broke apart into drifting world fragments suspended within a luminous cosmic medium that became known as the Aether Sea.

---

### [[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Sundrakar/The Aether Sea\|The Aether Sea]] and the Broken World

[[EN/Lore/Aethera - The World/Sundrakar/The Aether Sea\|The Aether Sea]] is not a true ocean. It is the glowing cosmic expanse born from the creation of the [[EN/Lore/History of Aethera/Worldveil\|Wordveil]] itself, a living medium that binds the shattered remnants of reality together.

Through it drift the broken pieces of the old world.

Ancient gateways and planar rifts can still open where the Worldveil weakens. Some appear only briefly, while others remain stable for centuries. The Sea became both road and barrier between worlds.

At the heart of this fractured reality remained two great continents:

[[Thalvëra\|Thalvëra]] and [[Sundrakar\|Sundrakar]].

[[Thalvëra\|Thalvëra]] became the stable heart of mortal civilization, while [[Sundrakar\|Sundrakar]] bore the deepest scars of the Shattering. The continent remained violent and unstable for centuries, surrounded by storms of raw Aether and uncontrolled planar fractures.

Even after the war ended, the world was still breaking apart.

---

### [[The Pact of the Veil\|The Pact of the Veil]]

The surviving gods remained in Aethera after the Shattering.

Together with the mortal races they began the long process later remembered as the Grand Rebuilding. They stabilized the Worldveil, sealed ancient rifts, calmed the Aether storms, and helped rebuild civilization from the ashes of the old world.

This era lasted for centuries.

The first kingdoms rose. Faiths and temples spread across the world. The foundations of mortal society were laid beneath divine guidance. During this time the gods made a final agreement among themselves known as the Pact of the Veil.

They understood that the world could not survive if gods continued shaping it directly forever. Too much divine interference risked drawing the attention of the Eidolons once more.

And so, one by one, the gods withdrew from the mortal world.

Some vanished quickly. Others remained for centuries longer. None truly know where they went. Some believe they journeyed beyond the Worldveil to search for new worlds. Others claim they hunt the [[Eidolons\|Eidolons]] still. A few faiths believe the gods merely sleep beyond reality, watching through dreams and omens.

Though absent, the gods never fully abandoned Aethera. Their chosen champions, prophets, and servants still carry fragments of divine will into the world.

---

### The Discovery of Aether

As [[Sundrakar\|Sundrakar]] slowly stabilized, mortals began to explore its shattered frontiers.

There they discovered Aether: a luminous substance born from the fractures of reality itself. Capable of fueling magic, shaping energy, and bending the laws of the world, Aether would forever transform civilization.

The stabilization of [[Sundrakar\|Sundrakar]] marked the true end of the Grand Rebuilding. For the first time since the Shattering, the world no longer stood on the edge of collapse.

The gods departed soon after.

And thus began the true Age of Mortals.

---

### The Sundering of the Elves

The elves suffered one of the greatest spiritual wounds after the destruction of Racriaven.

Many High Elves could never accept the loss of the original world. Believing that somewhere beyond the Worldveil the memory of true Racriaven still survived, most of their kind abandoned Aethera entirely and vanished into the depths of the Aether Sea in search of a new eternal homeland.

Only a few remained behind.

Some isolated themselves within ancient hidden cities in Thalvëra, preserving fragments of the old world and refusing contact with younger civilizations.

Others traveled to Sundrakar and dedicated themselves to the study of Aether itself. Over generations the energy of the continent transformed them. Their bodies, minds, and connection to magic changed until they became something entirely new.

These descendants would one day become known as the Dark Elves.

Most remaining elven peoples eventually became simpler and more closely tied to nature, giving rise to the Wood Elves who now wander the forests of Thalvëra.

---

### The Age of Dragons

For several centuries after the gods departed, the mortal races flourished. Kingdoms spread across Thalvëra while Sundrakar slowly became more habitable.

But the dragons endured.

Created during the Shattering War as living weapons against the Eidolons, dragonkind had spent centuries gathering strength within the distant mountain ranges of Sundrakar, particularly among the peaks of Drakhalor.

Division grew among them. Metallic dragons believed they should protect the younger races and preserve the balance of the world. Chromatic dragons increasingly saw mortals as weak inheritors of a world dragons had helped save.

Among them stood [[Tiamat\|Tiamat]].

Once noble, she was slowly corrupted by the influence of Bhaal, Myrkul, and Bane. Her mind twisted by pride, rage, and visions of draconic supremacy, she united many chromatic dragons beneath her banner.

When [[Bahamut\|Bahamut]] opposed her, she defeated and imprisoned him deep within the mountains of Drakhalor.

Then came the Dragon War.

For centuries dragons ruled vast regions of the world. Mortal kingdoms fell beneath flame and terror while [[Tiamat\|Tiamat]]’s dominion spread across Aethera.

Only when Bahamut was finally freed did the tides of war begin to change. Together with mortal champions and the metallic dragons, he confronted Tiamat in a final cataclysmic battle.

Tiamat was defeated and imprisoned within the same ancient prison where Bahamut himself had once been held.

The Age of Dragons ended.

---

### The Age of Reclaiming

Nearly eight hundred years have passed since the fall of [[Tiamat\|Tiamat]].

The modern kingdoms of Aethera rose from the ashes of the Dragon War. Trade routes spread across the continents. Aether became the foundation of magic, exploration, and civilization itself.

Most mortals now know the Shattering War only as fragmented myth. The Eidolons survive mainly in ancient texts, forbidden cults, and stories used to frighten children.

Yet the scars of the old wars remain everywhere.

The Worldveil still hums beyond reality.  
Ancient rifts still open beneath forgotten ruins.  
The Aether Sea still connects distant world fragments beyond mortal understanding.

And somewhere beyond the veil of creation, the Eidolons continue to wait patiently for the cycle to begin once more.
