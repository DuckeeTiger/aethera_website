> [!class-recite|wizard]
> *Weave Subtle Spells of Deception*
>
> You specialize in magic that dazzles the senses and tricks the mind.
>
> Through careful manipulation of perception, you create illusions that blur the line between reality and imagination.
>
> To an Illusionist, the impossible is merely something that has not yet been convincingly presented.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 3

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Illusion Savant
> ##### Level 3 Illusionist Feature
>
> Choose two Wizard spells from the Illusion school, each of which must be level 2 or lower.
>
> Add those spells to your spellbook for free.
>
> In addition, whenever you gain access to a new level of spell slots in this class, you can add one Illusion spell from the Wizard spell list to your spellbook for free.
>
> The chosen spell must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Improved Illusions
> ##### Level 3 Illusionist Feature
>
> Your mastery of illusion magic grants the following benefits:
>
> - **Silent Casting.** You can cast Illusion spells without providing Verbal components.
>
> - **Extended Range.** If an Illusion spell you cast has a range of 10 feet or greater, its range increases by 60 feet.
>
> - **Minor Illusion Mastery.** You learn the [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Minor Illusion\|Minor Illusion]] cantrip.
>
>   If you already know it, you instead learn a different Wizard cantrip of your choice.
>
>   This cantrip doesn't count against your number of cantrips known.
>
>   When you cast [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Minor Illusion\|Minor Illusion]]:
>
>   - You can create both a sound and an image with the same casting.
>   - You can cast it as a Bonus Action.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 6

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Phantasmal Creatures
> ##### Level 6 Illusionist Feature
>
> You always have [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Summon Beast\|Summon Beast]] and [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Summon Fey\|Summon Fey]] prepared.
>
> Whenever you cast either spell, you can change its school to Illusion, causing the summoned creature to appear spectral and illusory.
>
> You can cast the Illusion version of each spell without expending a spell slot.
>
> When cast in this way, the summoned creature has half its normal Hit Points.
>
> Once you cast either spell without a spell slot, you must finish a Long Rest before you can cast that spell in this way again.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 10

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Illusory Self
> ##### Level 10 Illusionist Feature
>
> When a creature hits you with an attack roll, you can take a Reaction to interpose an illusory duplicate of yourself.
>
> The triggering attack automatically misses you, and the illusion immediately dissipates.
>
> Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a Short or Long Rest.
>
> You can also restore a use of this feature by expending a level 2+ spell slot (no action required).

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 14

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Illusory Reality
> ##### Level 14 Illusionist Feature
>
> You have learned to weave shadow magic into your illusions, granting them a measure of reality.
>
> Whenever you cast an Illusion spell using a spell slot, you can choose one inanimate, nonmagical object that is part of the illusion and make it real.
>
> You can do so as a Bonus Action while the spell remains active.
>
> The object remains real for 1 minute.
>
> During that time, it:
>
> - Cannot deal damage.
> - Cannot impose conditions.
>
> For example, you could create the illusion of a bridge spanning a chasm and then make that bridge temporarily real.
