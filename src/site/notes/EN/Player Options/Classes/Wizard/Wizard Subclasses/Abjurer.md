> [!class-recite|wizard]
> *Shield Companions and Banish Foes*
>
> Your study of magic focuses on spells that block, banish, and protect.
>
> Abjurers specialize in ending harmful effects, banishing evil influences, and safeguarding the weak from magical threats.
>
> They are often sought when baleful spirits require exorcism, when important locations must be warded against magical intrusion, or when portals to other planes must be sealed.
>
> Adventuring parties prize Abjurers for the protection they provide against hostile magic and devastating attacks.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 3

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Abjuration Savant
> ##### Level 3 Abjurer Feature
>
> Choose two Wizard spells from the Abjuration school, each of which must be level 2 or lower.
>
> Add those spells to your spellbook for free.
>
> In addition, whenever you gain access to a new level of spell slots in this class, you can add one Abjuration spell from the Wizard spell list to your spellbook for free.
>
> The chosen spell must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Arcane Ward
> ##### Level 3 Abjurer Feature
>
> You can weave protective magic around yourself.
>
> When you cast an Abjuration spell using a spell slot, you can create a magical ward that lasts until you finish a Long Rest.
>
> The ward has a maximum number of Hit Points equal to:
>
> **(2 × your Wizard level) + your Intelligence modifier**
>
> Whenever you take damage, the ward takes the damage instead.
>
> If you have any Resistances or Vulnerabilities, apply them before reducing the ward’s Hit Points.
>
> If damage reduces the ward to 0 Hit Points, you take any remaining damage.
>
> While the ward has 0 Hit Points, it cannot absorb damage, though its magic remains.
>
> Whenever you cast an Abjuration spell using a spell slot, the ward regains Hit Points equal to twice the spell slot's level.
>
> Alternatively, as a Bonus Action, you can expend a spell slot to restore Hit Points to the ward equal to twice the expended slot's level.
>
> Once you create the ward, you can't create another one until you finish a Long Rest.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 6

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Projected Ward
> ##### Level 6 Abjurer Feature
>
> When a creature you can see within 30 feet of yourself takes damage, you can take a Reaction to have your Arcane Ward absorb that damage.
>
> If the damage reduces the ward to 0 Hit Points, the protected creature takes any remaining damage.
>
> If the creature has any Resistances or Vulnerabilities, apply them before reducing the ward’s Hit Points.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 10

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Spell Breaker
> ##### Level 10 Abjurer Feature
>
> You always have [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Counterspell\|Counterspell]] and [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Dispel Magic\|Dispel Magic]] prepared.
>
> In addition, you gain the following benefits:
>
> - You can cast [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Dispel Magic\|Dispel Magic]] as a Bonus Action.
> - You add your Proficiency Bonus to ability checks made for [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Dispel Magic\|Dispel Magic]].
> - When you cast either [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Counterspell\|Counterspell]] or [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Dispel Magic\|Dispel Magic]] using a spell slot, the slot isn't expended if the spell fails to stop its target.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 14

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Spell Resistance
> ##### Level 14 Abjurer Feature
>
> Your mastery of protective magic grants you extraordinary defenses.
>
> - You have Advantage on saving throws against spells.
> - You have Resistance to damage dealt by spells.
