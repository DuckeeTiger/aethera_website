> [!class-recite|wizard]
> *Wield Sword and Sorcery in Elegant Tandem*
>
> Bladesingers master a tradition of wizardry that incorporates swordplay and dance.  In combat, a Bladesinger uses intricate and elegant maneuvers that ward off harm while channeling magic into devastating attacks and cunning defenses. Many who witness a Bladesinger in battle remember the display as one of the most beautiful sights of their lives: a graceful dance accompanied by a singing blade.
>
> Bladesinging is strongly associated with ancient elven societies that first mastered the art and coined its name. Even today, most Bladesingers hail from old elven realms or lands shaped by elven traditions, carrying their talents across the world to aid common folk and perform heroic deeds.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 3

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Bladesong
> ##### Level 3 Bladesinger Feature
>
> As a Bonus Action, you invoke an elven magical technique known as the Bladesong, provided you aren't wearing armor or using a Shield.
>
> The Bladesong lasts for 1 minute and ends early if:
>
> - You gain the Incapacitated condition.
> - You don armor or a Shield.
> - You use two hands to make an attack with a weapon.
>
> You can dismiss the Bladesong at any time (no action required).
>
> While the Bladesong is active, you gain the following benefits:
>
> - **Agility.** You gain a bonus to your AC equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum of +1), and your Speed increases by 10 feet.
>
> - **Bladework.** Whenever you attack with a weapon with which you have proficiency, you can use your Intelligence modifier instead of Strength or Dexterity for attack and damage rolls.
>
> - **Focus.** Whenever you make a Constitution saving throw to maintain Concentration, you can add your Intelligence modifier to the roll.
>
> You can invoke the Bladesong a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum of once), and you regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Training in War and Song
> ##### Level 3 Bladesinger Feature
>
> You gain proficiency with all Martial Melee weapons that lack the Heavy and Two-Handed properties.
>
> You can also use any Melee weapon with which you have proficiency as a Spellcasting Focus for your Wizard spells.
>
> In addition, you gain proficiency in one of the following skills of your choice:
>
> - Acrobatics
> - Athletics
> - Performance
> - Persuasion

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 6

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Extra Attack
> ##### Level 6 Bladesinger Feature
>
> You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.
>
> Moreover, you can replace one of those attacks with the casting of a Wizard cantrip that has a casting time of one action.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 10

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Song of Defense
> ##### Level 10 Bladesinger Feature
>
> When you take damage while your Bladesong is active, you can take a Reaction to expend a spell slot and reduce the damage by an amount equal to five times the spell slot's level.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 14

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Song of Victory
> ##### Level 14 Bladesinger Feature
>
> After you cast a spell that has a casting time of an action, you can make one weapon attack as a Bonus Action.
