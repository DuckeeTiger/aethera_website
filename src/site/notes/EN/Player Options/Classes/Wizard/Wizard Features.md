> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Spellcasting
> ##### Level 1 Wizard Feature
>
> As a student of arcane magic, you have learned to cast spells. See the rules on spellcasting for details on how Wizard spells work.
>
> **Cantrips.** You know three Wizard cantrips of your choice. [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Light\|Light]], [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Mage Hand\|Mage Hand]], and [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Ray of Frost\|Ray of Frost]] are recommended. Whenever you finish a Long Rest, you can replace one of your cantrips from this feature with another Wizard cantrip of your choice.
>
> When you reach Wizard levels 4 and 10, you learn another Wizard cantrip of your choice, as shown in the Cantrips column of the Wizard Features table.
>
> **Spellbook.** Your wizardly apprenticeship culminated in the creation of a unique spellbook. It is a Tiny object that weighs 3 pounds, contains 100 pages, and can be read only by you or someone casting [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Identify\|Identify]].
>
> The book contains the level 1+ spells you know. It starts with six level 1 Wizard spells of your choice. [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Detect Magic\|Detect Magic]], [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Feather Fall\|Feather Fall]], [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Mage Armor\|Mage Armor]], [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Magic Missile\|Magic Missile]], [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Sleep\|Sleep]], and [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Thunderwave\|Thunderwave]] are recommended.
>
> Whenever you gain a Wizard level after level 1, add two Wizard spells of your choice to your spellbook. Each spell must be of a level for which you have spell slots.
>
> **Spell Slots.** The Wizard Features table shows how many spell slots you have to cast your level 1+ spells. You regain all expended spell slots when you finish a Long Rest.
>
> **Prepared Spells of Level 1+.** You prepare the list of level 1+ spells available for you to cast. To start, choose four spells from your spellbook. The chosen spells must be of a level for which you have spell slots.
>
> The number of prepared spells increases as you gain Wizard levels, as shown in the Prepared Spells column of the Wizard Features table.
>
> If another Wizard feature gives you spells that are always prepared, those spells don't count against the number of spells you can prepare.
>
> **Changing Your Prepared Spells.** Whenever you finish a Long Rest, you can change your prepared spells, replacing any of them with spells from your spellbook.
>
> **Spellcasting Ability.** Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for your Wizard spells.
>
> **Spellcasting Focus.** You can use an Arcane Focus or your spellbook as a Spellcasting Focus for your Wizard spells.
>
> **Expanding and Replacing a Spellbook**
>
> The spells you add as you gain levels represent ongoing magical research, but you can also discover additional spells during your adventures.
>
> **Copying a Spell into the Book.** When you find a level 1+ Wizard spell, you can copy it into your spellbook if it is of a level you can prepare and if you have time to copy it. Copying requires 2 hours and 50 GP per spell level.
>
> **Copying the Book.** You can copy spells from your spellbook into another book. This requires only 1 hour and 10 GP per spell level.
>
> If you lose your spellbook, you can recreate it by transcribing the Wizard spells you currently have prepared into a new spellbook.

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Ritual Adept
> ##### Level 1 Wizard Feature
>
> You can cast any spell as a Ritual if that spell has the Ritual tag and is in your spellbook. The spell doesn't need to be prepared, but you must read from your spellbook while casting it.

> [!class-divider|wizard small]
> ---

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Arcane Recovery
> ##### Level 1 Wizard Feature
>
> You can regain some of your magical energy by studying your spellbook.
>
> When you finish a Short Rest, you can recover expended spell slots with a combined level equal to no more than half your Wizard level (rounded up). None of the recovered slots can be level 6 or higher.
>
> Once you use this feature, you can't do so again until you finish a Long Rest.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 2

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Scholar
> ##### Level 2 Wizard Feature
>
> While studying magic, you specialized in another field of study.
>
> Choose one of the following skills in which you have proficiency:
>
> - Arcana
> - History
> - Investigation
> - Medicine
> - Nature
> - Religion
>
> You gain Expertise in the chosen skill.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 3

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Wizard Subclass
> ##### Level 3 Wizard Feature
>
> You gain a Wizard subclass of your choice. A subclass is a specialization that grants you features at certain Wizard levels.
>
> The following subclasses are available:
>
> - [[EN/Player Options/Classes/Wizard/Wizard Subclasses/Abjurer\|EN/Player Options/Classes/Wizard/Wizard Subclasses/Abjurer]]
> - [[EN/Player Options/Classes/Wizard/Wizard Subclasses/Diviner\|Diviner]]
> - [[EN/Player Options/Classes/Wizard/Wizard Subclasses/Evoker\|Evoker]]
> - [[EN/Player Options/Classes/Wizard/Wizard Subclasses/Illusionist\|Illusionist]]
> - [[EN/Player Options/Classes/Wizard/Wizard Subclasses/Bladesinger\|Bladesinger]]

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 4

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Ability Score Improvement
> ##### Level 4 Wizard Feature
>
> You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.
>
> You gain this feature again at Wizard levels 8, 12, and 16.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 5

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Memorize Spell
> ##### Level 5 Wizard Feature
>
> Whenever you finish a Short Rest, you can study your spellbook and replace one of the level 1+ Wizard spells you currently have prepared with another level 1+ spell from your spellbook.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 18

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Spell Mastery
> ##### Level 18 Wizard Feature
>
> You have achieved such mastery over certain spells that you can cast them at will.
>
> Choose one level 1 spell and one level 2 spell in your spellbook that have a casting time of one action.
>
> You always have those spells prepared, and you can cast them at their lowest level without expending a spell slot.
>
> To cast either spell at a higher level, you must expend a spell slot as normal.
>
> Whenever you finish a Long Rest, you can replace either chosen spell with another eligible spell of the same level from your spellbook.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 19

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Epic Boon
> ##### Level 19 Wizard Feature
>
> You gain an Epic Boon feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.
>
> [[EN/Player Options/Feats/All Feat/Boon of Spell Recall\|Boon of Spell Recall]] is recommended.

> [!class-level|wizard]
> Level 20

> [!class-feature|wizard]
> #### Signature Spells
> ##### Level 20 Wizard Feature
>
> Choose two level 3 spells in your spellbook as your signature spells.
>
> You always have these spells prepared, and you can cast each of them once at level 3 without expending a spell slot.
>
> After casting a spell this way, you can't cast it this way again until you finish a Short or Long Rest.
>
> To cast either spell at a higher level, you must expend a spell slot.
