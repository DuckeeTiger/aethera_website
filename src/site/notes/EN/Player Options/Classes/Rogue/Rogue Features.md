> [!class-level|rogue]
> Level 1

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Expertise
> ##### Level 1 Rogue Feature
>
> You gain Expertise in two of your skill proficiencies of your choice.
>
> *Sleight of Hand* and *Stealth* are recommended if you have proficiency in them.
>
> At Rogue level 6, you can choose two more skill proficiencies to gain Expertise in.

> [!class-divider|rogue small]
> ---

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Sneak Attack
> ##### Level 1 Rogue Feature
>
> You know how to strike subtly and exploit a foe's distraction.
>
> Once per turn, you can deal an extra 1d6 damage to one creature you hit with an attack if you have Advantage on the attack roll.
>
> The attack must use a Finesse or Ranged weapon.
>
> You don't need Advantage if:
>
> - At least one ally is within 5 feet of the target
> - The ally isn't Incapacitated
> - You don't have Disadvantage on the attack roll
>
> The extra damage increases with Rogue level, as shown in the Sneak Attack column of the Rogue Features table.

> [!class-divider|rogue small]
> ---

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Thieves' Cant
> ##### Level 1 Rogue Feature
>
> You picked up various languages in the communities where you plied your roguish talents.
>
> You know Thieves' Cant and one other language of your choice.

> [!class-divider|rogue small]
> ---

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Weapon Mastery
> ##### Level 1 Rogue Feature
>
> Your training allows you to use the mastery properties of two weapons of your choice that you're proficient with, such as Daggers and Shortswords.
>
> Whenever you finish a Long Rest, you can change the kinds of weapons you chose.

> [!class-level|rogue]
> Level 2

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Cunning Action
> ##### Level 2 Rogue Feature
>
> Your quick thinking and agility allow you to move and act quickly.
>
> On your turn, you can take one of the following actions as a Bonus Action:
>
> - Dash
> - Disengage
> - Hide

> [!class-level|rogue]
> Level 3

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Rogue Subclass
> ##### Level 3 Rogue Feature
>
> You gain a Rogue subclass of your choice.
>
> The following Rogue subclasses are available:
>
> - [[EN/Player Options/Classes/Rogue/Rogue Subclasses/Thief\|Thief]]
> - [[EN/Player Options/Classes/Rogue/Rogue Subclasses/Assassin\|Assassin]]
> - [[EN/Player Options/Classes/Rogue/Rogue Subclasses/Arcane Trickster\|Arcane Trickster]]
> - [[EN/Player Options/Classes/Rogue/Rogue Subclasses/Soulknife\|Soulknife]]

> [!class-divider|rogue small]
> ---

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Steady Aim
> ##### Level 3 Rogue Feature
>
> As a Bonus Action, you give yourself Advantage on your next attack roll on the current turn.
>
> You can use this feature only if you haven't moved during this turn, and after you use it, your Speed becomes 0 until the end of the current turn.

> [!class-level|rogue]
> Level 4

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Ability Score Improvement
> ##### Level 4 Rogue Feature
>
> You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.
>
> You gain this feature again at Rogue levels 8, 12, and 16.

> [!class-level|rogue]
> Level 5

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Cunning Strike
> ##### Level 5 Rogue Feature
>
> You've developed cunning ways to use your Sneak Attack.
>
> When you deal Sneak Attack damage, you can add one of the following effects:
>
> - **Poison (Cost: 1d6)**
> - **Trip (Cost: 1d6)**
> - **Withdraw (Cost: 1d6)**

> [!class-divider|rogue small]
> ---

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Uncanny Dodge
> ##### Level 5 Rogue Feature
>
> When an attacker that you can see hits you with an attack roll, you can take a Reaction to halve the attack's damage against you.

> [!class-level|rogue]
> Level 6

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Expertise
> ##### Level 6 Rogue Feature
>
> Choose two additional skill proficiencies.
>
> You gain Expertise in those skills.

> [!class-level|rogue]
> Level 7

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Evasion
> ##### Level 7 Rogue Feature
>
> When you're subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, you instead take no damage on a success and only half damage on a failure.
>
> You can't use this feature while Incapacitated.

> [!class-divider|rogue small]
> ---

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Reliable Talent
> ##### Level 7 Rogue Feature
>
> Whenever you make an ability check that uses one of your skill or tool proficiencies, you can treat a d20 roll of 9 or lower as a 10.

> [!class-level|rogue]
> Level 10

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Improved Cunning Strike
> ##### Level 11 Rogue Feature
>
> You can use up to two Cunning Strike effects when you deal Sneak Attack damage, paying the die cost for each effect.

> [!class-level|rogue]
> Level 13

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Devious Strikes
> ##### Level 14 Rogue Feature
>
> You gain the following additional Cunning Strike options:
>
> - **Daze (Cost: 2d6)**
> - **Knock Out (Cost: 6d6)**
> - **Obscure (Cost: 3d6)**

> [!class-level|rogue]
> Level 15

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Slippery Mind
> ##### Level 15 Rogue Feature
>
> Your cunning mind is exceptionally difficult to control.
>
> You gain proficiency in Wisdom and Charisma saving throws.

> [!class-level|rogue]
> Level 18

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Elusive
> ##### Level 18 Rogue Feature
>
> No attack roll can have Advantage against you unless you have the Incapacitated condition.

> [!class-level|rogue]
> Level 19

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Epic Boon
> ##### Level 19 Rogue Feature
>
> You gain an Epic Boon feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.
>
> Boon of the Night Spirit is recommended.

> [!class-level|rogue]
> Level 20

> [!class-feature|rogue]
> #### Stroke of Luck
> ##### Level 20 Rogue Feature
>
> If you fail a D20 Test, you can turn the roll into a 20.
>
> Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a Short or Long Rest.
