> [!class-recite|warlock]
> *Make a Deal with the Lower Planes*
>
> Your pact draws on the Lower Planes, realms of damnation, corruption, and ruin.
>
> You may have forged a bargain with a demon lord such as Demogorgon or Orcus, an archdevil like Asmodeus, or another mighty fiend of terrifying power.
>
> Whatever form your patron takes, its ultimate goals are dark and destructive.
>
> Your path is defined by how closely you serve those ambitions... or how fiercely you resist them.

> [!class-level|warlock]
> Level 3

> [!class-feature|warlock]
> #### Dark One's Blessing
> ##### Level 3 Fiend Patron Feature
>
> When you reduce an enemy to 0 Hit Points, you gain Temporary Hit Points equal to your Charisma modifier plus your Warlock level (minimum of 1 Temporary Hit Point).
>
> You also gain this benefit if another creature reduces an enemy within 10 feet of you to 0 Hit Points.

> [!class-feature|warlock]
> #### Dark One's Own Luck
> ##### Level 6 Fiend Patron Feature
>
> You can call upon your fiendish patron to bend fate in your favor.
>
> When you make an ability check or a saving throw, you can add 1d10 to the roll.
>
> You can do so after seeing the roll but before any of the roll's effects occur.
>
> You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum of once).
>
> You can use it no more than once per roll.
>
> You regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.

> [!class-level|warlock]
> Level 10

> [!class-feature|warlock]
> #### Fiendish Resilience
> ##### Level 10 Fiend Patron Feature
>
> Whenever you finish a Short or Long Rest, choose one damage type other than Force.
>
> You gain Resistance to that damage type until you choose a different one with this feature.

> [!class-level|warlock]
> Level 14

> [!class-feature|warlock]
> #### Hurl Through Hell
> ##### Level 14 Fiend Patron Feature
>
> Once per turn when you hit a creature with an attack roll, you can attempt to cast that creature through the horrors of the Lower Planes.
>
> The target must succeed on a Charisma saving throw against your spell save DC.
>
> On a failed save, the target disappears and hurtles through a nightmare landscape.
>
> If the target is not a Fiend, it takes 8d10 Psychic damage.
>
> The target also gains the [[EN/Player Options/Rules/Conditions/Incapacitated\|Incapacitated]] condition until the end of your next turn.
>
> It then returns to the space it previously occupied or the nearest unoccupied space.
>
> Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a Long Rest.
>
> You can also restore a use by expending a Pact Magic spell slot (no action required).
