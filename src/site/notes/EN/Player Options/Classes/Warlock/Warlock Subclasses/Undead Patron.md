> [!class-recite|warlock]
> *Defy Death for Profane Power*
>
> You've made a pact with a creature that defies the cycle of life and death: a powerful lich, a vampire, or another entity of undeath.
>
> Having once been mortal, these ancient patrons know firsthand the paths of ambition and the routes beyond death.
>
> They eagerly share this profane knowledge and other forbidden secrets with those who enact their will among the living.

> [!class-level|warlock]
> Level 3

> [!class-feature|warlock]
> #### Form of Dread
> ##### Level 3 Undead Patron Feature
>
> As a Bonus Action, you transform into an avatar of your patron's dreadful power.
>
> The transformation lasts for 1 minute, until you gain the Incapacitated condition, or until you end it (no action required).
>
> You can transform a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum of once), and you regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.
>
> While transformed, you gain the following benefits:
>
> - **Facsimile of Life.** You gain Temporary Hit Points equal to 1d10 plus your Warlock level.
>
> - **Fearless Form.** You have Immunity to the Frightened condition. If you are Frightened when you transform, that condition immediately ends.
>
> - **Frightful Avatar.** Once per turn, when you hit a creature with an attack roll, you can force it to make a Wisdom saving throw against your spell save DC.
>
>   On a failed save, the target has the Frightened condition until the end of your next turn.

> [!class-feature|warlock]
> #### Grave Touched
> ##### Level 6 Undead Patron Feature
>
> Your patron's powers have a profound effect on your body and magic.
>
> - **Arcane Necrosis.** Necrotic damage from your attacks, Warlock spells, and Warlock features ignores Resistance to Necrotic damage.
>
>   In addition, once per turn when you cast a spell that deals damage, you can change that spell's damage type to Necrotic.
>
> - **Dreaded Necrosis.** When you hit a creature with an attack roll and deal Necrotic damage while using your Form of Dread, you can roll one additional damage die when determining the Necrotic damage dealt.
>
>   You can use this benefit only once per turn.
>
> - **Undead Endurance.** You don't gain Exhaustion from dehydration, malnutrition, or suffocation.
>
>   In addition, you don't need to sleep, and magic can't put you to sleep.

> [!class-level|warlock]
> Level 10

> [!class-feature|warlock]
> #### Necrotic Husk
> ##### Level 10 Undead Patron Feature
>
> Your connection to undeath saturates your body, granting the following benefits:
>
> - **Necrotic Resilience.** You have Resistance to Necrotic damage.
>
>   While using your Form of Dread, you instead have Immunity to Necrotic damage.
>
> - **Unholy Resuscitation.** If you drop to 0 Hit Points and don't die outright, you can cause your body to erupt with deathly energy.
>
>   Each creature of your choice in a 30-foot Emanation originating from you must make a Constitution saving throw against your spell save DC.
>
>   On a failed save, a creature takes 2d10 plus your Charisma modifier Necrotic damage.
>
>   On a successful save, it takes half as much damage.
>
>   Your Hit Points then change to twice your Warlock level, and you gain 1 Exhaustion level.
>
>   Once you use this benefit, you can't do so again until you finish a Short or Long Rest.

> [!class-level|warlock]
> Level 14

> [!class-feature|warlock]
> #### Superior Dread
> ##### Level 14 Undead Patron Feature
>
> Your Form of Dread improves, granting you the following benefits while it is active:
>
> - **Dread Resistance.** You have Resistance to Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing damage.
>
> - **Ghostly Flight.** You gain a Fly Speed equal to your Speed and can hover.
>
>   You can move through creatures and objects as if they were Difficult Terrain, but you take 1d10 Force damage if you end your turn inside a creature or an object.
>
> - **Profane Casting.** Whenever you cast a Warlock spell from the Conjuration or Necromancy school, you cast it without Verbal, Somatic, or Material components.
>
>   This benefit doesn't remove Material components that have a cost or are consumed by the spell.
