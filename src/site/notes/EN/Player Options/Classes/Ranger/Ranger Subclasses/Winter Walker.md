> [!class-recite|ranger]
> *Withstand the Horrors of the Frigid Wasteland*
>
> Winter Walkers hone their craft in the bleak and frozen wilds of places such as Icewind Dale.
>
> Rimed with ice and hardened by endless winters, these ruthless Rangers hunt the monsters that stalk arctic wastes, eventually becoming chilling terrors themselves.
>
> More than any other Rangers of the frozen north, Winter Walkers are versed in the region’s strange phenomena, from the lingering magic of fallen Netherese cities to the threats lurking beneath the ice and deep underground.
>
> Some believe their powers are a curse bestowed by nature spirits. Others claim they are forever marked by exposure to ancient evils.
>
> Whatever the truth, Winter Walkers inspire equal measures of respect and fear.

> [!class-level|ranger]
> Level 3

> [!class-feature|ranger]
> #### Frigid Explorer
> ##### Level 3 Winter Walker Feature
>
> You gain the following benefits:
>
> - **Frost Resistance.** You have Resistance to Cold damage.
>
> - **Polar Strikes.** Once per turn when you hit a creature with a weapon, you can deal an extra **1d4 Cold damage** to the target. This extra damage ignores Resistance to Cold damage. When you reach Ranger level 11, the extra damage increases to **1d6**.

> [!class-divider|ranger small]
> ---

> [!class-feature|ranger]
> #### Hunter's Rime
> ##### Level 3 Winter Walker Feature
>
> Ice rimes both you and your prey, protecting you while hindering your quarry.
>
> Whenever you cast [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Hunter's Mark\|Hunter's Mark]], you gain Temporary Hit Points equal to **1d10 + your Ranger level**.
>
> In addition, while a creature is marked by your Hunter's Mark, it can't take the Disengage action.

> Level 7

> [!class-feature|ranger]
> #### Fortifying Soul
> ##### Level 7 Winter Walker Feature
>
> Your experience surviving the harshest environments allows you to bolster your companions.
>
> When you finish a Short Rest, choose a number of creatures you can see equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of one).
>
> Each chosen creature:
>
> - Regains Hit Points equal to **1d10 + your Ranger level**.
> - Gains Advantage on saving throws to avoid or end the Frightened condition for 1 hour.
>
> Once you use this feature, you can't do so again until you finish a Long Rest.

> [!class-level|ranger]
> Level 11

> [!class-feature|ranger]
> #### Chilling Retribution
> ##### Level 11 Winter Walker Feature
>
> When a creature hits you with an attack roll, you can take a Reaction to force it to make a Wisdom saving throw against your spell save DC.
>
> On a failed save, the creature gains the Frightened condition until the end of your next turn.
>
> While Frightened in this way, its Speed is reduced to 0.
>
> You can use this Reaction a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of once), and you regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.

> [!class-level|ranger]
> Level 15

> [!class-feature|ranger]
> #### Frozen Haunt
> ##### Level 15 Winter Walker Feature
>
> When you cast [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Hunter's Mark\|Hunter's Mark]], you can assume a ghostly, snow-laden form.
>
> This form lasts until the spell ends.
>
> Once you use this feature, you can't do so again until you finish a Long Rest unless you expend a level 4+ spell slot (no action required).
>
> While in this form, you gain the following benefits:
>
> - **Frozen Soul.**
>
>   You have Immunity to Cold damage.
>
>   When you first assume this form and at the start of each of your turns thereafter, each creature of your choice in a 15-foot Emanation originating from you takes **2d4 Cold damage**.
>
> - **Partially Incorporeal.**
>
>   You have Immunity to the Grappled, Prone, and Restrained conditions.
>
>   You can move through creatures and objects as though they were Difficult Terrain.
>
>   If you end your turn inside a creature or object, you take **1d10 Force damage**.
>
>   If this form ends while you are inside a creature or object, you are shunted to the nearest unoccupied space.
