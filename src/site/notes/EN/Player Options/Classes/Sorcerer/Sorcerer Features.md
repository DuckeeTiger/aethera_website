> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Spellcasting
> ##### Level 1 Sorcerer Feature
>
> Drawing from your innate magic, you can cast spells. See the Player’s Handbook for the rules on spellcasting. The information below details how you use those rules with Sorcerer spells.
>
> **Cantrips.** You know four Sorcerer cantrips of your choice. [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Light\|Light]], [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Prestidigitation\|Prestidigitation]], [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Shocking Grasp\|Shocking Grasp]], and [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Sorcerous Burst\|Sorcerous Burst]] are recommended.
>
> Whenever you gain a Sorcerer level, you can replace one of your cantrips from this feature with another Sorcerer cantrip of your choice.
>
> When you reach Sorcerer levels 4 and 10, you learn another Sorcerer cantrip of your choice.
>
> **Spell Slots.** The Sorcerer Features table shows how many spell slots you have to cast your level 1+ spells. You regain all expended slots when you finish a Long Rest.
>
> **Prepared Spells of Level 1+.** You prepare the list of level 1+ spells that are available for you to cast with this feature. To start, choose two level 1 Sorcerer spells. [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Burning Hands\|Burning Hands]] and [[EN/Player Options/Spells/Detailed Spells/Detect Magic\|Detect Magic]] are recommended.
>
> The number of spells on your list increases as you gain Sorcerer levels, as shown in the Prepared Spells column of the Sorcerer Features table.
>
> Whenever that number increases, choose additional Sorcerer spells until the number of spells on your list matches the number in the table.
>
> If another Sorcerer feature gives you spells that you always have prepared, those spells don’t count against the number of spells you can prepare with this feature.
>
> **Changing Your Prepared Spells.** Whenever you gain a Sorcerer level, you can replace one spell on your list with another Sorcerer spell for which you have spell slots.
>
> **Spellcasting Ability.** Charisma is your spellcasting ability for your Sorcerer spells.
>
> **Spellcasting Focus.** You can use an Arcane Focus as a Spellcasting Focus for your Sorcerer spells.

> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Innate Sorcery
> ##### Level 1 Sorcerer Feature
>
> An event in your past left an indelible mark on you, infusing you with simmering magic.
>
> As a Bonus Action, you can unleash that magic for 1 minute. During that time:
>
> - The spell save DC of your Sorcerer spells increases by 1.
> - You have Advantage on attack rolls made with Sorcerer spells.
>
> You can use this feature twice, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.

> [!class-level|sorcerer]
> Level 2

> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Font of Magic
> ##### Level 2 Sorcerer Feature
>
> You gain Sorcery Points, which fuel certain Sorcerer features.
>
> Your number of Sorcery Points is shown in the Sorcery Points column of the Sorcerer Features table.
>
> You regain all expended Sorcery Points when you finish a Long Rest.

> [!class-divider|sorcerer small]
> ---

> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Metamagic
> ##### Level 2 Sorcerer Feature
>
> Your magic flows from within, allowing you to alter spells to suit your needs.
>
> You gain two Metamagic options of your choice from the Metamagic Options list.
>
> To use a Metamagic option, you spend the required number of Sorcery Points.
>
> You can use only one Metamagic option on a spell unless the option specifically states otherwise.
>
> Whenever you gain a Sorcerer level, you can replace one Metamagic option you know with another.
>
> You gain two additional Metamagic options at Sorcerer levels 10 and 17.

> [!class-level|sorcerer]
> Level 3

> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Sorcerer Subclass
> ##### Level 3 Sorcerer Feature
>
> You gain a Sorcerer subclass of your choice.
>
> You can choose from the following Sorcerer Subclasses:
>
> - [[EN/Player Options/Classes/Sorcerer/Sorcerer Sublasses/Aberrant Sorcery\|Aberrant Sorcery]]
> - [[EN/Player Options/Classes/Sorcerer/Sorcerer Sublasses/Clockwork Sorcery\|Clockwork Sorcery]]
> - [[EN/Player Options/Classes/Sorcerer/Sorcerer Sublasses/Draconic Sorcery\|Draconic Sorcery]]
> - [[EN/Player Options/Classes/Sorcerer/Sorcerer Sublasses/Wild Magic Sorcery\|Wild Magic Sorcery]]

> [!class-level|sorcerer]
> Level 4

> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Ability Score Improvement
> ##### Level 4 Sorcerer Feature
>
> You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.
>
> You gain this feature again at Sorcerer levels 8, 12, and 16.

> [!class-level|sorcerer]
> Level 5

> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Sorcerous Restoration
> ##### Level 5 Sorcerer Feature
>
> When you finish a Short Rest, you can regain expended Sorcery Points equal to up to half your Sorcerer level (round down).
>
> Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a Long Rest.

> [!class-level|sorcerer]
> Level 7

> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Sorcery Incarnate
> ##### Level 7 Sorcerer Feature
>
> If you have no uses of Innate Sorcery remaining, you can activate it by spending 2 Sorcery Points when taking the Bonus Action to activate the feature.
>
> In addition, while Innate Sorcery is active, you can use up to two Metamagic options on each spell you cast.

> [!class-level|sorcerer]
> Level 19

> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Epic Boon
> ##### Level 19 Sorcerer Feature
>
> You gain an Epic Boon feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.
>
> Boon of Dimensional Travel is recommended.

> [!class-level|sorcerer]
> Level 20

> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Arcane Apotheosis
> ##### Level 20 Sorcerer Feature
>
> While your Innate Sorcery feature is active, you can use one Metamagic option on each of your turns without spending Sorcery Points on it.
