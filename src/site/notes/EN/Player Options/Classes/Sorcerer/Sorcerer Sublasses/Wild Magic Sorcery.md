> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Wild Magic Surge
> ##### Level 3 Wild Magic Sorcery Feature
>
> Once per turn, immediately after you cast a Sorcerer spell using a spell slot, you can roll 1d20.
>
> If you roll a 20, roll on the Wild Magic Surge table to determine a magical effect.
>
> If the resulting effect casts a spell, that spell cannot be modified by Metamagic.

> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Tides of Chaos
> ##### Level 3 Wild Magic Sorcery Feature
>
> You can manipulate chaos itself to gain Advantage on one D20 Test before you roll.
>
> Once you do so, you must either cast a Sorcerer spell using a spell slot or finish a Long Rest before using this feature again.
>
> If you cast a Sorcerer spell with a spell slot before finishing a Long Rest, you automatically roll on the Wild Magic Surge table.

> [!class-level|sorcerer]
> Level 6

> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Bend Luck
> ##### Level 6 Wild Magic Sorcery Feature
>
> Immediately after another creature you can see rolls a D20 Test, you can take a Reaction and spend 1 Sorcery Point.
>
> Roll 1d4 and apply the result as either a bonus or penalty (your choice) to that roll.

> [!class-level|sorcerer]
> Level 14

> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Controlled Chaos
> ##### Level 14 Wild Magic Sorcery Feature
>
> Whenever you roll on the Wild Magic Surge table, you can roll twice and choose either result.

> [!class-level|sorcerer]
> Level 18

> [!class-feature|sorcerer]
> #### Tamed Surge
> ##### Level 18 Wild Magic Sorcery Feature
>
> Immediately after you cast a Sorcerer spell using a spell slot, you can choose an effect from the Wild Magic Surge table instead of rolling for one.
>
> You can choose any result except the final row (97–00).
>
> If the chosen effect requires a roll, you must still make that roll normally.
>
> Once you use this feature, you can't do so again until you finish a Long Rest.
